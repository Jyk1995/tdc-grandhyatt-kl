The Dinner Club started in a Singapore apartment with no guest list, no PR, and no plan. Stefan cooked. Strangers came. By the time dessert was down, they were already making plans for the week after.

That instinct became something intentional — and something bigger than we expected. People who met at our very first supper clubs are still a group today. They came as strangers. They left as friends. Now they come back together, as a table, because The Dinner Club is where their friendship started.

Stefan trained across five Michelin-starred kitchens — including Ciel Bleu at Okura Hotel Amsterdam and La Rive at InterContinental Amstel. He knows how hotel kitchens work, and how to make them feel like anything but. His most recent role was Head Chef at Somma (Lo & Behold Group), recognised by Michelin Guide Singapore, Tatler Asia Pacific, and Condé Nast Traveler in 2025.

The concept

What we're proposing

The Dinner Club
comes to Kuala Lumpur

Two nights. Two long tables. A supper club format that doesn't exist in KL yet — one long table where strangers sit next to strangers, a multi-course chef's menu unfolds, and by the end of the evening the room is one conversation. This is what The Dinner Club does. This is what we're bringing to Grand Hyatt KL.

Night 01
Supper Club — Night One
Open waitlist. Full seating. One or two long tables — everyone faces each other, no private groups, no separation. The format is intentional: it forces the kind of conversation that only happens when strangers share a table with nowhere to hide. Media and press welcome as guests.
Night 02
Supper Club — Night Two
By Night Two, the people at the first dinner are already talking. Word travels fast when a room full of strangers ends the evening as friends. The second night fills itself — and it fills with people who already know they want to be there.
The menu
Multi-course chef's menu
Stefan and the Grand Hyatt KL kitchen team create a menu together — honest about where it comes from. Singapore sensibility, KL ingredients, European technique. Every dish has a reason. The table learns it as the night unfolds. One seating per night. 25–40 covers.
The design
Long table. One conversation.
No round tables. No safe corners for groups who already know each other. One long table — or two — so the whole room is part of one evening. This is the design. By dessert, a guest at one end is laughing at something a stranger said at the other. That's not luck. That's the format.
The food

Modern European.
Asian soul.

Every dish tells you where it came from. European technique, Asian instinct, honest ingredients. Nothing on the plate without a reason.

How we get the room full

Marketing that
doesn't feel like marketing

What we do
  • Social-first announcement — teaser post from @thedinnerclub.sg + @stefankam before a single ticket goes on sale. The waitlist fills before we open anything.
  • Behind-the-scenes content — Stefan and the Grand Hyatt KL chefs in the kitchen together. Two chef worlds colliding is a story people share.
  • Press and PR — KL food press, lifestyle media, hotel trade coverage. Media guests join as part of the evening, not as a separate function.
  • Cross-border reach — Singapore audience sees the KL dates. KL audience sees the Singapore pedigree. Both sell the other.
  • Waitlist strategy — opens 3 weeks before. Scarcity drives demand. If it sells out faster than expected, that's the story.
What we don't do
  • No "exclusive" or "curated" language — this isn't a velvet rope. The copy sounds like a smart friend telling you about the best dinner they've ever been to.
  • No hotel-brochure photography — content is candid, warm, real. Plating shots, kitchen chaos, the moment someone laughs. Not staged wide shots of an empty room.
  • No generic event listings — we don't list on aggregator sites. Discovery is word of mouth and social. That's what keeps the waitlist honest.
  • Food and experience first, always — every piece of content leads with the meal, the people, and the evening. That's what travels. That's what sells the next seat.
