Who we are

We aren't a brand.
We're a table.

The Dinner Club started in a Singapore apartment with no guest list, no PR, and no plan. Stefan cooked. Wendy hosted. Strangers came. By the time dessert was down, they were already friends.

That instinct became something intentional. Every dinner since has been the same: real food, real conversation, the kind of evening people talk about for months. Every seat, sold out — every time.

Stefan trained across five Michelin-starred kitchens — including Ciel Bleu at Okura Hotel Amsterdam and La Rive at InterContinental Amstel. He knows how hotel kitchens work, and how to make them feel like anything but a hotel. His most recent role was Head Chef at Somma (Lo & Behold Group), recognised by Michelin Guide Singapore, Tatler Asia Pacific, and Condé Nast Traveler in 2025.

The concept

What we're proposing

The Dinner Club
comes to Kuala Lumpur

Two nights. Two seatings. A supper club format that doesn't exist in KL yet — intimate, chef-led, ticketed, full. Not a pop-up. Not an event. A room that feels like the best dinner party you've ever been to, at a hotel that knows how to hold that energy.

Night 01
Supper Club — Night One
Open waitlist. Full seating. One or two long tables, everyone facing each other — no round tables, no separation. The format forces connection in a way a restaurant never could. Media and press are welcome to join as guests.
Night 02
Supper Club — Night Two
A second night means a second chance to fill the room with people who heard about the first. Waitlist overflow, word of mouth from the night before. By Night Two, the buzz is already doing the work.
Format
Multi-course chef's menu
Stefan works with the Grand Hyatt KL kitchen team to create a menu that's honest about where it comes from — Singapore sensibility, KL ingredients, European technique. One seating per night. 25–40 covers.
The table
Long table. One conversation.
The Dinner Club is built around one or two long tables — not a room of separate parties. Strangers sit next to strangers. The format is the magic. By dessert, the whole table is one conversation.
The guest journey

What it feels
like to be there

The Dinner Club doesn't feel like a restaurant dinner. It doesn't feel like a hotel event. It feels like being let in on something. Every detail is designed to make the table feel like the only table in the world.

01
The arrival
Guests don't walk into a restaurant. They walk into a room that's already warm. Music low, candles lit, the kitchen already moving. The atmosphere says: you're exactly where you're supposed to be.
02
The welcome
Wendy hosts. Stefan introduces. Not a speech — a conversation. The kind of table talk that makes strangers turn to each other before the first course is down.
03
The menu
Multi-course, story-driven. Stefan and the Grand Hyatt KL chef team cook together. Each dish has a reason. The table learns it as the night unfolds.
04
The moment
Somewhere between the main and dessert, the room changes. The volume lifts. People who didn't know each other an hour ago are making plans for next week. That's the whole point.
05
The close
No rush. No bill-drop energy. The evening ends when the room decides it should. Guests leave with something to talk about. That's how the waitlist for the next one fills itself.

How we get the room full

Marketing that
doesn't feel like marketing

What we do
  • Social-first announcement — teaser post from @thedinnerclub.sg + @stefankam before a single ticket goes on sale. The waitlist fills before we open anything.
  • Behind-the-scenes content — Stefan and the Grand Hyatt KL chefs in the kitchen together. This is the content that travels. Two chef worlds colliding is a story people share.
  • Press and PR — KL food press, lifestyle media, hotel trade coverage. Night 01 with industry guests becomes the press drop.
  • Cross-border reach — Singapore audience sees the KL dates. KL audience sees the Singapore pedigree. Both sell the other.
  • Waitlist strategy — opens 3 weeks before. Scarcity drives demand. No discount, no early-bird. If it sells out faster than expected, that's the story.
What we don't do
  • No "exclusive" or "curated" language — this isn't a velvet rope. The copy sounds like a smart friend telling you about the best dinner they've ever been to.
  • No hotel-brochure photography — content is candid, warm, real. Plating shots, kitchen chaos, the moment someone laughs at a table. Not staged wide shots of an empty room.
  • No generic event listings — we don't list on aggregator sites. Discovery is word of mouth and social. That's what keeps the waitlist honest.
  • Food and experience first, always — every piece of content leads with the meal, the people, and the evening. That's what travels. That's what sells the next seat.

What makes this possible

What we need
from Grand Hyatt KL

We bring the concept, the content, the chef, the host, and the audience. Here's what the partnership needs from your side to make two nights of KL's best supper club happen.

01
Space & atmosphere
A private dining room or configurable space that seats 25–40 for two evenings. Lighting that can go low. A room that can feel like it belongs to the guests for the night.
02
Kitchen access & collaboration
Stefan works alongside the Grand Hyatt KL kitchen team. Joint menu development, shared prep time in the days before. The collaboration is real — not symbolic.
03
F&B partnership
Front-of-house service managed by Grand Hyatt's team. F&B (including wine and drinks available on the night) structured as a revenue-share or fixed arrangement — open to discussion.
04
Co-promotion
Grand Hyatt KL shares across their owned channels — social, email, in-hotel. Not alcohol-led. Focused on the experience, the chefs, and the format. We handle the full content creation.
